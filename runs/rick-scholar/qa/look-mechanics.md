# Rick Scholar Look Mechanics

The planted shoes and lower coat hem stay anchored. His physical eyes lead each gaze: pupils and eyelids move with the eye globes, then the head and upper torso follow with a restrained turn. Spiky hair and coat collar lag one small step. The handheld portal scanner remains attached to the right hand and lags subtly during sideways turns.

- `000 up`: eyes lift, eyelids open slightly, chin and scanner tip raise.
- `090 screen-right`: nose, gaze, head, torso, and scanner aim toward screen-right; left side of face is more visible.
- `180 down`: eyes and chin lower, brows compress, scanner tilts down slightly.
- `270 screen-left`: nose, gaze, head, torso, and scanner aim toward screen-left; right side of face is more visible.

Every 22.5-degree step uses even eye/head/body movement. No whole-sprite rotation, scaling, or raster warp.
